About

In 2011, I graduated from Clemson University with a B.A. in History. While at Clemson, I also took a couple of semesters in Graphic Communication courses. I have always enjoyed drawing and would consider it one of my favorite hobbies. After graduation, I begin volunteering with KidSpring, the children’s ministry at NewSpring Church, and was eventually brought in as a full time staff member. While with KidSpring, I learned to hone and craft my illustration ability under the guidance of two incredible artists. Also, my time at KidSpring was a great experience in working together and collaborating daily on a team of 14. I had heard through a friend about a new coding school that had opened up in Greenville called The Iron Yard. Web development has always been something I have been really interested in. I had a little experience with Wordpress type sites and some basic HTML/CSS, but I wanted to be better. The Iron Yard was just the opportunity I needed to gain some real programming knowledge. So I began class in the Front-end Academy in August 2013. Under the guidance and instruction of great teachers and mentors (Mason Stewart and Eric Dodds), I learned skills and grew as a programmer in ways that I never could have on my own. The Iron Yard also taught me important things like the culture of web development and the best and standard practices in the front-end world. I’m currently looking for an opportunity to continue to grow, improve and expand my developing skills.
